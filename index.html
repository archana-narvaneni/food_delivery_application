It's no wonder that parrots are one of the world's favorite pets.
They are colorful, quite intelligent, highly sociable, and long-lived creatures.
Different Parrot species vary largely in characteristics such as color, weight, and habits.
There are close to 400 parrot species around the globe, but sadly, many of them have become endangered. Here are five interesting facts about parrots you should know.

💗

Many birds grip food in their feet, but only Parrots can hold their food up to their beaks whilst they eat.
The smallest parrot is the buff-faced pygmy parrot, which is only about 3 inches.
Many species are monogamous and spend their lives with only one mate.
Parrots are smart Parrots can imitate sounds
Parrots learn well, and they learn fast. If you haven't already witnessed it, know that parrots are one of the most intelligent birds out there.
But not all of them can fly They can eat with their feet
They are capable of associating words with objects and situations, and can even use tools and solve problems.
Parrots live long
Some scientists argue that they have the IQ equivalent to that of a four-year-old. What's more? Parrots are quite playful.
